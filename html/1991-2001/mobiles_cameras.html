First Mobile Phone with a Camera


Altough they do not compare to today's smarthphones, the first mobile phones with cameras were a huge milestone in the journey our phones have taken to become contemporary iPhones and Samsungs.

Asia leading the way


The first mobile phone with a camera was the J-SH04, produced by Sharp Corporation for SoftBank Mobile in Japan in November 2000. It had a 110,000-pixel camera with digital zoom and a 256-color display.



There were two other competitor devices: the Korean Samsung SCH-V200, also released in November 2000, and the Japanese Kyocera VP-210 Visual Phone, released in May 2000.

Although both the competitors also had a camera their functionality was not equal to a fully celluar device with integrated camera and thus the Mobile Phone Museum considers the J-SH04 to be the first actual phone with a camera. The SCH-V200 had to transfer the photos by cable to the actual device and VP-210 was a DECT-based PHS device, not a cellular device.

Don't give up - Sharp didn't


The phone has an interesting backstory to it. The idea for a mobile phone with a camera was proposed by the producent company - Sharp. Sharp approached the biggest mobile provider in Japan - NTT DoCoMo, but it was rejected. Then they prosposed the idea to the second biggest network provider KDDI, but they also didn't want to invest in the idea. Finally, Sharp approached the smallest of the network providers - SoftBank Mobile.

The company was divided about the project. The engineers were excited about the idea but the marketers were not that optimistic citing the fact that the phone had a small screen and a low-resolution camera. In the end Softbank Mobile decided to order 10.000 devices but only 2000 of those would be the new camera equipped phones.

The phone was huge success. It sold out within two weeks and the 8000 devices without the cameras were still on the shelves. It was one of the first real world showcases of the popularity and the potential of phones with integrated cameras.
