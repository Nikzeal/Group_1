Huge Apple Expansion


The year 2001 came with a lot of changes for Apple. They released their first iPod, a new Mac OS X operating system and iTunes. Physical Apple retail stores were announced.

Intoducing the new iPod


On October 23, 2001, during a special event, Steve Jobs introduced the first iPod with all its features. It was able to store up to impressive 1,000 songs and had a 5GB hard drive and a 2-inch monochrome display.



The device turned out to be a huge success. It was the first portable music player that was able to store so many songs. It was also the first device that used the famous scroll wheel. It is estimated that Apple sold over 450 million iPods between the launch in 2001 and the line discontinuation on May 10, 2022.

Mac OS X - Apples new operating system


In March of 2001, Apple officialy released their new operating system called Mac OS X the successor of the Mac OS 9. The first version was code-named Cheetah.



The new operating system was a huge step forward for Apple. It was built on technology made by NeXT, a company that was founded by Steve Jobs after he left Apple in 1985. NeXT was acquired by Apple in 1997 and Steve Jobs returned to Apple.



The new OS had many new features and improvements. It was the first OS to support Apple's new Aqua user interface. It also had a new Finder, a new Dock and a new Mail application. Not all was great about it though. The Mac OS X 10.0 was criticized for its slow speed, lack of features and bugs.

Introducing iTunes


January 9, 2001 gave the World the announcement of iTunes, a media player and library application. It was originally developed as SoundJam MP by Casady & Greene and later renamed iTunes when the company was acquired by Apple in 2000. The app was gradually improved and new features were added to it like support for digital video, podcasts, ebooks and more.

Apple comes to retail


On May 19, 2001, Apple opened its first two retail stores in Tysons Corner, Virginia (shown in the picture below) and Glendale, California. The stores were designed by Ron Johnson and Steve Jobs to improve the buying experience for customers. On July 18, 2001 Steve Jobs announced that the first days of the stores were met with terrific interest from the people and that Apple will be expanding its retail store network to 25 by the end of the year.



The success of the stores and Apple as a whole is best described by the fact that Apple stores are the most profitiable stores in the world in terms of sales per square foot with about $5,546 of revenue each square foot of store space.
