Birth of Google

Google, the company established in 1998, and the world’s most famous search engine, trace their roots back to a project called “BackRub”. BackRub was a research project of two PhD students, Larry Page and Sergey Brin, at Stanford University in California. The programmer who wrote a lot of the original code for the Google Search engine but left before Google become a company, Scoot Hassan, is considered an unofficial “third founder."


Project Start

Larry Page was considering various topics for his dissertation theme, but the one decided to go with involved exploring the mathematical aspects of the World Wide Web and understanding its link structure as a vast graph. He focused specifically on the issue of determining which web pages are linked to a particular page. He believed that this information, including the number and nature of backlinks, could provide valuable insights about a page, drawing inspiration from the role of citations in academic publishing. Larry Page then shared his ideas with Hassan, who took the initiative to write the code necessary to implement these ideas.


The PageRank Algorithm

The PageRank algorithm was developed by Brin and Page to analyze link data. The algorithm considers pages referenced the most by other pages as relevant. They observed that a search engine based on PageRank would yield better outcomes compared to existing techniques of web search, which primarily ranked results based on the frequency of search terms appearing on a page. Convinced that pages with the highest number of backlinks from highly relevant web pages would be the most relevant in searches, Page and Brin conducted tests to validate their hypothesis and established the basis for their search engine.


Project Release and the Founding of Google

The initial release BackRub occurred in August 1996 on the Stanford website. Soon after, Backrub was renamed Google. The name was a play on the mathematical expression for the number 1 followed by 100 zeros. On September 15, 1997, the domain google.com was officially registered. On September 4, 1998, Google was formally incorporated as a company in Menlo Park, California, in the garage of their friend Susan Wojcicki.
