Release of BitTorrent


In the year 2001 the world was introduced to BitTorrent. Bram Cohen's invention turned out to be a revolution in the world of file sharing.

What is BitTorrent?


BitTorrent is a peer-to-peer file sharing protocol created and released by a programmer named Bram Cohen on 2nd of July 2001. It focuses on sharing large files. The protocol is dividing the file into smaller pieces and then shares them between the peers(users) in the network. Then every time a peer wants to download the original file it will be downloaded from multiple other peers with the small pieces of the file.

That decentralization of the file sharing process is the main advantage of the BitTorrent protocol. The lack of a central server makes sharing large files faster and run better especially on networks with limited bandwidth.

To use torrents you need a torrent client. The client is a program that can read the torrent file containing all the important metadata about the file you want to download and use it to download it from other peers.

The impact of torrent


BitTorrent has changed the way we share files. It has become one of the most popular ways of sharing large files. In 2019 BitTorrent was responsible for 27% of all upstream and 2.46% downstream traffic on the internet. (that's huge)

Although BitTorrent is a great innovation it has also contributed to the spread of piracy. It's ability to shar large files quickly and efficiently makes it a perfect tool to share pirated movies, tv series, games and music. One of the most popular websites with pirated content - The Pirate Bay is using (among others) the BitTorrent protocol to share files.



The decentrailzed nature and popularity of piracy are a dividing topic. Some people see information as something that should be free and available to everyone. Others see piracy as a threat to the entertainment industry and the economy. That's why BitTorrent was, is and probably will be a controversial topic.
