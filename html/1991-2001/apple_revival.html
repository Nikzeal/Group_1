Revival of Apple

In August 1997, Apple and Microsoft made the decision to move forward and leave the past behind. During that year's Macworld event, Steve Jobs and Bill Gates announced a groundbreaking agreement between the two companies.


Situation of Apple in the 1990s

During the late 1980s and the 1990s, Apple found itself in a challenging situation due to increased competition from IBM and Microsoft. Both companies had successfully launched their own operating systems and computer products. In an attempt to keep up with these rivals, Apple made a series of unfortunate business decisions. In the early 1990s, they released new products, such as the Newton PDA and the Macintosh LC, which were not well-received by consumers and failed to generate sales. As a result of these setbacks, Apple's financial situation worsened, leading to significant layoffs and the closure of multiple facilities. Many believed that the company was on the brink of bankruptcy.

However, in 1997, Steve Jobs returned as the CEO of Apple, bringing with him a fresh vision for the company's future.


Microsoft's investment in Apple

At this critical moment, Microsoft unexpectedly came to Apple's rescue. Despite being fierce rivals, Microsoft made a significant investment in Apple by purchasing $150 million of Apple stock in August 1997. Both companies benefited greatly from the agreement. Apple obtained important financial resources, and Microsoft managed to support a weakened competitor, thereby addressing concerns about its monopolistic power over the market. The investment also resolved an ongoing lawsuit where Apple argued that Microsoft had used elements of the Mac GUI in its Windows operating system. Furthermore, Microsoft also promised to support Microsoft Office for the Macintosh for 5 years while Apple agreed to make Internet Explorer the default web browser on the Mac.
