What happened during this period?

From 1968 to 1979, the informatics field underwent transformative changes. The development of ARPANET in 1969 marked the inception of what would become the modern Internet. This period also witnessed a shift in computer hardware, from large mainframes to more accessible minicomputers and eventually personal computers, exemplified by the Apple II and Commodore PET. Programming languages like C and SQL were developed, laying the groundwork for future software. The 1968 NATO Software Engineering Conference heralded the formalization of software engineering as a discipline. The relational database model, proposed in 1970, revolutionized data storage and retrieval. Advancements in computer networking, especially with the development of TCP/IP, and the nascent field of computer graphics, including GUIs and devices like the mouse, made computers more user-friendly. These years set the stage for the computer-dominated world we live in today, with significant leaps in both hardware and software technologies.
