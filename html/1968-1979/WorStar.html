What is WordStar?

WordStar is a microcomputer word processing application that was invented in 1978 by businessman and software developer Seymour I. Rubinstein and Rob Barnaby.This software allows users to write and update documents, create text layouts, and preview the printed version of the document on the monitor. WordStar became popular around the domain of personal computer software due to its user friendly interface. This whole idea started in IMS Associates INC, where Seymour Rubinstein, and R. Barnaby got in contact. However in the late 1970â€™s, Rubinstein left IMSAI to found his own company called MicroPro International Inc, where he persuaded Barnaby to join the project he had in mind of developing two different softwares:

WordMaster (an editor)
SuperSort (a sorting program)

However as soon as these two projects were done, Barnaby started working on a new project idea by Rubinstein, WordStar. On September 1978, version 1 of WordStar for CP/M was released.

The minds behind the software

Rob Barnaby and Seymour Rubinstein

Seymour Ivan Rubinstein

Seymour Ivan Rubinstein was an American entrepreneur and software developer born in 1934. Rubinstein is known to be the man who led the business aspect of his industry MicroPro Associates Inc. and the man who came up with the idea for WordStar. He started his career and first got involved with software development in IMSAI, on which he was the marketing head of the company and an assembly code programmer.

Rob Barnaby

Rob Barnaby was the lead programmer at MicroPro and is acknowledge to be the main developer of WordStar. His main contribution to the project was designing the code for WordStar and the future development and upgrades of the software, however, he is also a relevant in figure for the CP/M operating system since in his early career, he worked as a developer to enhance features of CP/M.

How does WordStar work?

WordStar is a command based word processor software, in which users interact with the application by inserting pre-defined commands. This application was designed for the CP/M operating system, which in the early days, was implemented very commonly in personal computers. In this application, users could easily scroll and travel through documents and editing them sticking to the commands provided by the software, and therefore being able to produce better looking and more professional documents.

Relevance and Legacy

WordStar became a pioneer application in the field of work processing software, due to its various features and concepts that it implemented, and nowadays are standard in the field of text editors and word processing tools. One of the characteristics that led to its big influence and therefore to a long lasting legacy is the fact that it was standardised just a few years after its development, which influenced a lot on the way people approached the processes of creating and editing a text document. Finally, its user interface was very likeable and user friendly, therefore many modern word processing softwares take bases from WordStar to develop a linkable user interface.
