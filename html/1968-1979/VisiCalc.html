What is VisiCalc?

VisiCalc was the first spreadsheet computer program for personal computers, which was released in 1979 for the Apple II home computer. This program was characterised by having pretty straightforward structure and use. The main purpose of VisiCalc was to allow data sorting and storing in tabular rows and columns, which came very handy to replace the manual operation of developing and managing spreadsheets. This program was crucial since a single change on a cell was automatically applied to all the spreadsheet, making the process of data sorting and storage a lot easier and insanely faster. According to Steve Jobs, VisiCalc, commonly known as the “killer app”, was one of the reasons that led the Apple II to have the success it had.

At the time, small businesses and entrepreneurs saw this program as an amazing way to boost productivity by reducing the amount of time it took them to organise and alter information about their business, which made them buy personal computers just to have access to this program.

The minds behind the program

Bob Frankston and Dan Bricklin

Dan Bricklin

Dan Bricklin is a computer scientist and entrepreneur born on July 16, 1951 in the United States. Bricklin obtained a double career title in electrical engineering and informatics at the MIT After graduation, he worked for the Digital Equipment Corporation (DEC) where he acquired a vast knowledge regarding software development and business, which motivated him to return to college at Hardvard to obtain a master in Business administration, where he started and co-developed the VisiCalc program.

Bob Frankston

Bob Frankston is an American software engineer born in June 14th, 1949. He studied his bachelor in software engineering at the MIT where he met Dan Bricklin, and did a master of Engeneering in computer science at the same institution. He participated as a co-founder of VisiCalc in 1979.

How does VisiCalc work?

VisiCalc is an electronic spreadsheet that allows users to organise data in rows in columns, where users can perform calculations and create dynamic models by linking cells together. The way a user inputs data in this program is through cells, in which they can preform mathematical operations between different cells by inputing formulas that link a cell to another, which implies a single change on a cell automatically updates all the information that is linked to that cell.

Relevance and Legacy

VisiCalc revolutionised the way businesses managed their numerical and statistical data. With VisiCalc implemented, a single home computer was valuable to a business, making this way of data administration cheaper and faster. This revolutionised the business world by giving a new value to personal computers, and allowing people to give a practical use to these devices for a faster, more efficient, and user friendly performance.
