General info

Game of life by Sam Twidale

Conway's Game of Life is a cellular automaton devised by British mathematician John Conway in 1970. The Game of Life is not a traditional game with players, but rather a simulation that evolves over time based on a set of simple rules. It is a zero-player game, meaning that its progression is determined solely by its initial state.

The idea of John Conway

Cellular automata

The idea for the Game of Life came to John Conway as he was interested in exploring the concept of cellular automataâ€”a mathematical model that consists of a grid of cells, each in one of a finite number of states, and evolves through a series of discrete time steps based on a set of rules. Conway sought to create a cellular automaton with simple rules that could generate complex and interesting patterns.

Game rules

The Game of Life is played on an infinite two-dimensional grid of square cells, where each cell can be in one of two states: alive or dead. The state of a cell evolves over discrete generations, following a set of rules:

Game of life simple example



  • Birth: A dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes alive (reproduction).
  • Survival: A live cell with two or three live neighbors stays alive.
  • Death: A live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies (underpopulation), or with more than three live neighbors dies (overpopulation).

Conway's rules create dynamic and intricate patterns that can be stable, oscillating, or evolving. Despite the simplicity of the rules, the Game of Life exhibits complex behaviors, and many interesting patterns and structures have been discovered, including gliders, oscillators, and spaceships.

Conclusion

The consequences of Conway's Game of Life extend beyond its original purpose as an experiment in cellular automata. It has become a significant topic of study in computer science, mathematics, and artificial life. The Game of Life has applications in various fields, such as cryptography, image processing, and even music composition. Its simplicity and universality make it a popular subject for exploration and experimentation in computer science and mathematical research. The Game of Life has also inspired the development of other cellular automata and has had a lasting impact on the study of complex systems and emergent behavior.
