/ Valve's big changes in gaming /

During 2021, many changes came to be. In this year, Valve had something in store for us... in particular, an announcement. Was it good, was it bad? This cannot be decided univocally. During 2021, Valve announced that it would no longer support Blockchain games on it's steam platform, on twitter. The company's position about it was as follows: "Steam's point of view is that items have value and they don't allow items that can have real-world value on their platform." but, altough important, the most important change was in the gaming industry, and, in particular, in the hardware section. Thanks to Valve, who released it's own console: The Valve Steam Deck! This console was functional, handheld, and, most importantly, had and still has hardware components that would teach other companies some lessons... here they are!

/ Tesla's A. I. Cars! /

Have you ever dreamt, as a child, to be James Bond, Kim possible, or some other sort of super spy? Maybe Batman, or Tony Stark, even. Have you ever dreamt of being inside the Batmobile? Of being Inspector Gadget? Well, during 2021, Tesla had got you covered! In previous years, for its products, Tesla used NVIDIA chips, but in 2021 they unveiled their custom, silicon made, Tesla D1 chip! This allowed for something great, and, up until then, only dreamed of: Self driving, autopilot cars! How cool is that? The chip revolutionised their technology, and was a great step forward with bringing A. I. into people's lives.
