Launch of Wikipedia

Wikipedia, one of the world's most important online encyclopedias, has seen exponential growth since it entered the market, displacing its 'predecessor' Nupedia and bringing in content even from space with a vocal from Italian astronaut Paulo Nespoli. Probably one of the keys to its success were the rules for the addition of content by the community, which however also led to real problems such as "The Seigenthaler incident".

The introduction of WI-FI

Wi-Fi, developed in the 1980s, gained widespread adoption in the early 2000s, revolutionized internet access by providing cable-free connection. It played a key role in the work and beyond. However, with increased usage came security concerns, as hackers exploited vulnerabilities in public networks. Given the increasing importance of protecting oneself from cyber-attacks when connected to the Internet, VPN technologies have also developed. In 2014, a security experiment highlighted the vulnerability of public Wi-Fi networks, emphasizing the need for user awareness and data protection measures.
