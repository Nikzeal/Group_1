CEO Opposition

Initially, the CEO of Google was opposed to the idea of developing a web browser. He said that at the time, Google was a "small company" and did not want to go through a browser war.

After hiring developers who previously worked on the Firefox browser, they built a prototype and the CEO said that "it was so good that it essentially forced me to change my mind"

Introduction to the market

In September 2004, rumours that Google was building a web browser first appeared. Journalists noticed that Google was starting to hire former Microsoft developers. Google introduced Google Chrome in September 2008. A 38-page comic by Scott McCloud was sent to various journalists. to explain the features within the new browser

Naming

"Chrome" was an initial development project code name, because it is associated with fast cars and speed. Google then decided to keep the name. The name "Chrome" also doesn't contain any cultural or religious references, as well as being easy to understand and pronounce in any language.

Key Features

Google Chrome contained numerous new features that haven't been seen in any other browser before, such as a new tab page that displayed the most visited sites. The browser quickly gained popularity due to its minimalistic and sleek interface, as well as becoming renowned amongst users for its performance. It introduced a new JavaScript engine called V8, designed to execute JavaScript code more quickly than other browsers. Unlike other browsers, Google Chrome treated each tab as a separate process. This means if one tab crashed, it wouldn't necessarily affect the entire browser.

Rise to dominance

Chrome quickly gained about 1% market share. After release, market share then kept dropping until it hit a low of 0.69% in October 2008. It then started rising again and by December 2008, Chrome again passed 1% market share. Chrome overtook Firefox in November 2011. Chrome now has 67% desktop market share, making it the most popular web browser. The success of Chrome has been attributed to its speed, performance, and the integration of other popular Google services.
