What is it?

Cloud computing is when computer resources can be acquired on-demand without the need for access to the physical hardware. Users typically pay for the service through a pay-as-you-go model, where you are billed for the time and amount of resources utilised. This means you are not paying for unused resources.

History

Cloud computing was first seen in the 1960s, where users could submit jobs to be run on mainframe computers. In 2002, Amazon Web Services was released to the public and is one of the largest cloud computing platforms today. Since the COVID-19 pandemic, cloud computing has surged in popularity as businesses and employees had to stay home and cloud computing allowed for remote working.

Cloud Gaming

Services like GeForce Now allow users to play games with a better experience than their own hardware. It operates in a similar manner to remote desktop software, but with a focus on lower latency. It eliminates the need to purchase and maintain expensive computer systems. It typically requires a stable, high-speed connection.

Advantages of Cloud Computing

Cloud Computing means businesses no longer have to invest in hardware and infrastructure, whilst only paying for the exact time they utilise resources. It also means that businesses can take advantage of being able to almost instantly scale up or down, allowing greater ability to adapt to current conditions. Cloud providers often have robust backup systems in place, meaning users no longer have to maintain the backups themselves.
