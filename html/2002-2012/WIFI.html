The invention of the WIFI(1991)

WIFI was born out of the need to connect devices to other devices and to the Internet without the use of cables (wireless) and it was 1985 when large companies designed systems to send data without cables. It was not until 1991 that it was used for the first time by telephone companies. The range of Wi-Fi was very small compared to todays in fact you almost always had to be attached to the router for the device to connect.

Public WIFI(2002)

In the 2000s, Wi-Fi gained significant global relevance, influencing several sectors, including business, education and mobile devices. Although the technology was developed in the late 1980s and 1990s, its introduction and standardization took off mainly in the early years of the new millennium. Wi-Fi has revolutionized Internet access, enabling wireless connections that have improved flexibility and mobility. By 2002, Wi-Fi adoption was on the rise, with an increased presence in public places, allowing users to connect to the Internet without the use of cables. This period saw an increase in Wi-Fi usage in corporate settings, where wireless connectivity simplified operations and improved productivity. Educational institutions have also adopted Wi-Fi technology to provide Internet access to students and facilitate online resources.

WIFI Security

Hackers use various techniques to exploit public networks and gain unauthorized access to data. Packet 'sniffing' intercepts unencrypted data, while man-in-the-middle attacks manipulate communications between victim and server. Also, the trap of a connection to a FakeFreeWIFI is very dangerous. Spoofing,Phishing, Heartbleed attacks, allows hackers to gain unauthorized access. To defend oneself, one should use VPNs and keep one's device constantly updated.

Wouter Slotboom

Wouter Slotboom is a Dutch computer security expert and poker player who in 2014 did an experiment inside a cafe in Amsterdam. The experiment consisted of using the coffeeshop's wifi network and using a black device the size of a cigarette packet to access all customer data. He succeeded in doing this in only 20 minutes, obtaining all the data he had set himself. He did not at all want to hack all the customers composed for evil purposes but rather to make them realize how insecure our data is, it only takes one person who is able to use and read tools like the one he used to get all the information he wanted without any effort.
