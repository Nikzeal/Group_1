The Release Of Google Map

Google Maps was officially launched on February 8, 2005.
Google acquired Where 2 Technologies in October 2004, which laid the foundation for the development of Google Maps. The acquisition brought key talent and technology into Google's fold, contributing to the creation of the mapping platform.
Google Maps was developed by a team led by Danish-American software engineer Lars Rasmussen and his brother Jens Eilstrup Rasmussen. The development team worked at Where 2 Technologies, a company that was acquired by Google in 2004.

Earlier features and their consequences

The initial version of Google Maps allowed users to view maps, get directions, and search for businesses and points of interest. It featured a draggable map interface, which was a novel and intuitive way for users to navigate the map. Google Maps was tightly integrated with Google's local search functionality, allowing users to find businesses, services, and locations directly on the map. One of the groundbreaking features of Google Maps was the inclusion of satellite imagery. Users could switch to a satellite view to see aerial images of locations. The "Hybrid" view, which overlaid street maps with satellite imagery, provided users with a unique and detailed perspective.

Continious Development and Global Expansion

Following its launch, Google Maps underwent continuous development and improvement. Regular updates introduced new features, improved accuracy, and expanded coverage. In 2007, Google introduced Street View, a feature that allowed users to virtually explore and navigate streets through panoramic images. Street View was initially launched in a few cities but expanded rapidly to cover a wide range of locations globally. Google Maps became available on mobile devices, including smartphones, enabling users to access maps and directions while on the go. Over the years, Google Maps expanded its coverage to include maps and local information for countries and regions worldwide.
