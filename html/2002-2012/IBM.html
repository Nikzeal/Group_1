The Importance Of Supercomputer

The word Supercomputer has a funny reason behind its invention. In fact, became mainstream when IBM's Deep Blue supercomputer challenged the world chess champion in two tournaments around the world.
But what is a Supercomputer?
A supercomputer is a device that has the best capabilities around, in terms of computing speed and memory. Supercomputers are useful for:
- Problems that deals with quantum physics
- Also, Molecular Modeling, the biggest weather forecast Agencies, and physicist has one for the highly level computation needed by their work.

Four Generation of BlueGene

BlueGene/L(2004)
  • Was Needed for High-Level Computation System
  • It uses an massive parallel processing architecture
  • Due to the small low-power chips that uses called "Nodes", It has a good Energy Efficency.
BlueGene/C(2007)

It was an extension of the previous one that had an enhanced architecture with the latest technology and better performances but nothing more

BlueGene/P(2007)

  • It is a modular hybrid parallel system and has an enormous amounts of Nodes(2048)
  • Engaged to try to resolve the most difficult problem of his time
  • With this we can say that the boundary between the computational science and engineering was very thin
BlueGene/Q(2012)

  • Continued the tradition of a highly scalable parallel computing architecture
  • It uses an massive parallel processing architecture
  • Enable the simulation and analysis of complex scientific phenomena and has the ability to reach a wide range of scientific research applications
  • Also the Energy Efficiency was at his best for his time.
