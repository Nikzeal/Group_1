Skype(2003)

Was invented in the 2003 from a group of an Estonian developers. The name stands for “Sky Peer to Peer”, It is an online communication application that allows users to make voice and video calls, send instant messages, and share files over the Internet. It offers the ability to make calls to landlines and mobile phones worldwide, facilitates voice and video conferencing, and enables file and screen sharing

Skype 1.0(2003)

Skype 1.0 represents the first public version of Skype, an online communication service launched in 2003. This release introduced key features such as free voice calls between users, low-cost calls to landlines and mobile phones, instant messaging and file sharing. The initial user interface was simple but intuitive. Skype 1.0 quickly gained popularity for its ability to offer free communication services over the Internet, overcoming geographical barriers. Over the years, Skype evolved, incorporating new features such as group video calling and integrating with Microsoft, which acquired Skype in 2011. The platform has had a significant impact in both the personal and business context, consolidating itself as a major option for online communication.

Skype 2.0(2004)

Skype 2.0 maintains its clean interface but has undergone some polishing. The main new feature is video, which you plan to examine shortly. Two notable features are the real-time contact search, allowing users to find contacts by typing names into the dialing field, and the ability to group contacts. Grouping contacts enables organization and easy initiation of conference calls, chats, or file sharing with the entire group in one click. However, you mentioned that Groups are turned off by default, and you had to enable them. Adding contacts to groups is a straightforward drag-and-drop process.

Integration with Facebook and a redesigned user interface linking the contacts in the other social network, this almost monopolized the network world as it was enough to have these two applications to be connected to a huge community.

The DownFall Of Skype

The downfall of Skype as the main communication platform was caused by several key factors. First, the rise of competitors such as Zoom and Microsoft Teams and no innovation on the other side. After being acquired by Microsoft in 2011, attention and investment shifted to Microsoft Teams, reducing development on Skype. Security and privacy issues over the years further damaged the platform's reputation. In parallel, changes in user preferences, with increased adoption of platforms integrated into work and social media services, contributed to Skype's decline.
