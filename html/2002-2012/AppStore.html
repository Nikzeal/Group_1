What is it?

The App Store is an app marketplace developed by Apple for mobile apps on the iPhone and iPad, as well as MacOS. It allows users to download apps approved by Apple.

Release

The App Store was released on July 10, 2008 with the release of the iPhone 3G and the 2nd gen iPod Touch. It initially had 500 applications available to download. It now features more than 1.8 million apps. It received major redesigns in 2013 and 2016.

iOS SDK

Steve Jobs initially did not intend to provide the ability for 3rd party developers to create applications for iOS. After major backlash, Apple relented and released the iOS SDK on March 6, 2008. In 2021, Apple introduced a feature which required apps to request user consent for data tracking.

Criticism and Monopoly allegations

The App Store has attracted criticism from both individual developers and the EU. They allege that as the App Store is the only way for consumers to install applications onto their devices, Apple is performing unfair practices by taking a 30% cut of all sales through the App Store. Apple is now preparing to allow users to download alternative app stores.
